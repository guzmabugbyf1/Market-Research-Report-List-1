<p><p>Brain-Computer Interface (BCI) technology enables direct communication between the brain and external devices, facilitating control and interaction without conventional inputs. The BCI market is broadly categorized into two types: invasive and non-invasive. Invasive BCIs involve surgical implantation of devices within the brain, offering high accuracy and signal fidelity, primarily for medical applications like paralysis treatment. Non-invasive BCIs use external sensors to capture brain signals, favored for their safety and ease of use in consumer and gaming applications.</p></p>
