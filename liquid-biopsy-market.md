<p><p>Liquid biopsy is a minimally invasive diagnostic technique that involves the analysis of circulating tumor cells, cell-free DNA, or exosomes in blood or other bodily fluids to detect cancer and monitor treatment response. This approach provides valuable insights into tumor genetics and dynamics without the need for traditional tissue biopsies, making it increasingly popular in oncology.</p><p>The liquid biopsy market is experiencing significant growth, driven by advancements in technology, an increase in cancer prevalence, and a shift towards personalized medicine. As healthcare continues to emphasize early detection and real-time monitoring of disease progression, liquid biopsies are becoming essential tools for oncologists. </p><p>The Liquid Biopsy Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 5.6% during the forecast period. Key trends include the emergence of innovative technologies such as next-generation sequencing and digital PCR, which enhance the sensitivity and specificity of liquid biopsies. Additionally, the rising focus on companion diagnostics and the integration of artificial intelligence in data analysis are facilitating more accurate interpretations and fostering further market expansion. Collaborations between research institutions and biotech companies are also intensifying, pushing the boundaries of liquid biopsy applications in oncology and other fields.</p></p>
